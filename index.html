Position Analysis

As illustrated in these charts, there's a significant disparity in the values of football players from different nations. For instance, British players collectively boast a market value exceeding 100 billion euros—one of the highest globally—while Chinese players together barely reach 5 million euros. Such volatility in football valuations suggests focusing more on relative rather than absolute values. The evolution of football tactics, from early formations like the WM to modern setups such as 4-4-2 and 3-5-2, has profoundly impacted the sport. It's led to the emergence of iconic players like Maradona, Beckenbauer, and Cruyff, who each set standards for playing various positions.

Size: Echoing the Olympic motto "higher, faster, stronger," an athlete's physical dimensions significantly impact their potential. Regrettably, a player as short as 150 cm faces steep challenges in achieving stardom without exceptional effort. Typically, forwards range from 175 to 190 cm, with the most successful often around 185 cm. Their height enhances their ability in aerial duels, making headers a potent tactic. Midfielders, however, have less stringent size requirements as their roles demand agility and the ability to handle pressure from multiple directions more than sheer speed. Defenders mirror forwards in their physical attributes—height and strength aid in combating opposing forwards. Goalkeepers, notably tall, benefit from their stature as it allows them to cover a greater area of the goal, crucial for defending against free kicks. This trend towards taller goalkeepers is gradually limiting opportunities for shorter goalkeepers.

Weight: Corresponds with height; heavier players often leverage their mass to control space and the ball effectively. Yet, optimal performance typically results from a balanced ratio of height to weight, which maximizes value and efficacy on the pitch.

This graph highlights how player values and career longevity correlate with their positions and ages. For instance, Kylian Mbappé and Erling Haaland, representing the midfielders and forwards, are prized for their youth and vigor. In contrast, defenders and goalkeepers like Giorgio Chiellini and Manuel Neuer see their experience valued as they age. Seasoned veterans such as Lionel Messi and Cristiano Ronaldo defy these general trends by maintaining their market value and extending their playing years through intensive training and consistent high performance.

Skill moves in football reflect a range of techniques utilized by players. While midfielders and forwards often showcase more elaborate skills—rated between 4 and 5 on the skill scale—due to their roles in creating and scoring goals, it is essential to recognize that defenders and goalkeepers also possess a high degree of proficiency. Their skill set is simply more attuned to the defensive aspects of the game.

The 1-to-5 scale represents ordinal levels of skill moves where a score of 1 or 2 doesn't signify a lack of ability, but rather a different focus in gameplay. High skill ratings, indicative of advanced ball-handling abilities, command greater market value, especially for positions where such traits are in high demand.

This graph illustrates the distribution of weak foot ability across various positions in football. Typically, midfielders and forwards benefit more from having a strong weak foot as it enhances their versatility and unpredictability. While a rating from 1 to 5 indicates a spectrum of capability, ratings of 4 or 5 often signal superior skill in ball control and are highly valued. Notably, seasoned players like Kylian Mbappé, and Lionel Messi exemplify the impact of high weak foot ratings, which contribute to their substantial market value.
